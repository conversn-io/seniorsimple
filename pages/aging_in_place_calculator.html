Aging in Place Guide

Make your home senior-friendly and plan for aging in place with confidence. Complete guide to modifications, safety tips, and cost considerations.

Why Aging in Place Matters

Aging in place allows you to remain in your own home and community as you age, maintaining independence and familiarity. With proper planning and modifications, your home can adapt to your changing needs over time.

Did you know? 90% of adults over 65 want to stay in their homes as they age, according to AARP research.

Key Benefits

  • Maintain independence and autonomy
  • Stay connected to your community
  • Often more cost-effective than moving
  • Preserve emotional connections to your home
  • Maintain familiar routines and relationships

Home Assessment Checklist

Before making modifications, assess your home's current accessibility and safety. Use this checklist to identify areas that need attention.

Safety Hazards

  • Loose rugs or carpeting
  • Poor lighting in hallways and stairs
  • Cluttered walkways
  • Slippery surfaces in bathrooms
  • Missing or loose handrails
  • High thresholds between rooms

Accessibility Needs

  • Doorway width (minimum 32 inches)
  • Step-free entries
  • Accessible bathroom and shower
  • Reachable light switches and controls
  • First-floor bedroom and bathroom

Comfort & Convenience

  • Easy-to-use door handles and faucets
  • Adequate storage at reachable heights
  • Comfortable seating areas
  • Temperature control accessibility
  • Good ventilation and air quality

Pro Tip

Consider hiring a Certified Aging-in-Place Specialist (CAPS) for a professional assessment. They can provide personalized recommendations based on your specific needs and home layout.

Your Home, Your Independence

Aging in place is about more than just staying in your home—it's about maintaining your independence, comfort, and quality of life. With proper planning and the right modifications, your home can adapt to your changing needs while keeping you safe and comfortable.

Remember: The best time to make modifications is before you need them. Start with safety essentials and build from there.
