Understanding Power of Attorney

What is a Power of Attorney?

A power of attorney is a legal document that allows you to appoint someone you trust (called an "agent" or "attorney-in-fact") to make decisions on your behalf if you become unable to do so yourself.

Key Benefits:

  • • Ensures your affairs are managed according to your wishes
  • • Avoids court-appointed guardianship
  • • Provides peace of mind for you and your family
  • • Can be tailored to your specific needs

Types of Power of Attorney

Financial Power of Attorney

Manages financial and business affairs

Healthcare Power of Attorney

Makes medical and healthcare decisions

Durable Power of Attorney

Remains in effect if you become incapacitated

Financial Power of Attorney

Powers Typically Granted

Real estate transactions

Important Considerations

  • • Choose a highly trustworthy agent
  • • Consider naming a successor agent
  • • Specify limitations if desired
  • • Review and update regularly
  • • Keep original documents safe
  • • Notify relevant institutions

DURABLE POWER OF ATTORNEY FOR FINANCES

State of: ________________

SECTION 1: PRINCIPAL

I, _________________________ (full name), residing at _________________________ (address), City: _____________, State: _______, ZIP: _______, being of sound mind, hereby appoint:

SECTION 2: AGENT

_________________________ (full name), residing at _________________________ (address), City: _____________, State: _______, ZIP: _______, Phone: _____________, as my agent (attorney-in-fact) to act for me in any lawful way with respect to my financial affairs.

SECTION 3: SUCCESSOR AGENT

If my agent is unable or unwilling to serve, I appoint _________________________ (full name), residing at _________________________ (address), as my successor agent.

SECTION 4: POWERS GRANTED

I grant my agent the power to perform the following acts on my behalf:

□ Banking and financial institution transactions

□ Real estate transactions

□ Stock, bond, and commodity transactions

□ Insurance and annuity transactions

□ Tax matters

□ Business operating transactions

□ Legal proceedings

□ Other: _________________________

SECTION 5: DURABILITY

This power of attorney shall remain in effect even if I become incapacitated or mentally incompetent.

  • • Discuss your values and wishes with your agent

  • • Choose someone who can make difficult decisions

Healthcare Power of Attorney Form Template

HEALTHCARE POWER OF ATTORNEY

State of: ________________

SECTION 1: APPOINTMENT OF AGENT

I, _________________________ (full name), residing at _________________________ (address), hereby appoint _________________________ (agent's name) as my agent to make healthcare decisions for me when I am unable to make them for myself.

SECTION 2: ALTERNATE AGENT

If my primary agent is unable to serve, I appoint _________________________ (alternate agent's name) as my alternate agent.

SECTION 3: POWERS OF AGENT

My agent has the authority to:

□ Make all healthcare decisions for me

□ Consent to or refuse medical treatment

□ Choose healthcare providers and facilities

□ Access my medical records

□ Make decisions about life-sustaining treatment

□ Authorize medical procedures and surgery

□ Make decisions about mental health treatment

SECTION 4: GUIDANCE FOR AGENT

In making healthcare decisions for me, my agent should consider:

□ My personal values and beliefs

□ My previously expressed wishes

□ The benefits and risks of treatment

□ My quality of life preferences

SECTION 5: EFFECTIVE DATE

This power of attorney becomes effective when my attending physician determines that I lack the capacity to make healthcare decisions.

State-Specific Requirements

Important Legal Notice

Power of attorney laws vary by state. Always consult with an attorney in your state to ensure compliance with local requirements.

Common State Requirements

Notarization

Most states require notarization of financial POAs

Witnesses

Some states require one or more witnesses

Specific Language

Certain states require specific statutory language

Recording

Real estate POAs may need to be recorded

Special Considerations

HIPAA Authorization

Include HIPAA authorization for medical records access

Financial Institution Forms

Banks may require their own POA forms

Agent Limitations

Some states limit who can serve as an agent

Multiple States

Consider separate POAs if you own property in multiple states
