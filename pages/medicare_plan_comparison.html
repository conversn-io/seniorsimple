Popular Plan Types

Lower costs, primary care physician required, referrals needed

PPO (Preferred Provider Organization)

More flexibility, can see out-of-network providers at higher cost

SNP (Special Needs Plans)

Medicare Supplement Plan Types

Plan F (if eligible)

Most comprehensive coverage, covers all gaps in Original Medicare

Plan G

Comprehensive coverage, you pay Medicare Part B deductible

Plan N

Good coverage with small copayments for some services

Which Option is Right for You?

Consider Medicare Advantage if:

  • You want prescription drug coverage included
  • You prefer lower monthly premiums
  • You want extra benefits like dental, vision, or wellness programs
  • You're comfortable with network restrictions
  • You don't mind getting referrals for specialists

Consider Medicare Supplement if:

  • You want freedom to choose any Medicare provider
  • You prefer predictable out-of-pocket costs
  • You travel frequently and want coverage nationwide
  • You don't want to worry about referrals
  • You're willing to pay higher premiums for more flexibility
