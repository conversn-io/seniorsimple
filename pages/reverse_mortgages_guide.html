Understanding Reverse Mortgages

A comprehensive look at reverse mortgages, including pros, cons, eligibility requirements, and who they're right for.

12 min read
Intermediate
Housing

Key Takeaway

A reverse mortgage allows homeowners 62 and older to convert part of their home equity into cash without selling their home or taking on monthly mortgage payments. However, it's not right for everyone and comes with significant considerations.

What is a Reverse Mortgage?

A reverse mortgage is a loan that allows homeowners aged 62 and older to access the equity in their homes without having to make monthly mortgage payments. Instead of paying the lender, the lender pays you, either in a lump sum, monthly payments, or a line of credit.

Important to Know

The most common type of reverse mortgage is the Home Equity Conversion Mortgage (HECM), which is insured by the Federal Housing Administration (FHA).

Eligibility Requirements

Age & Ownership

At least 62 years old
Own your home outright or have low mortgage balance
Live in the home as your primary residence

Financial & Property

Demonstrate financial capability for ongoing costs
Property must meet FHA standards
Complete HUD-approved counseling

Important Consideration

You must continue to pay property taxes, homeowners insurance, and maintain the home. Failure to do so can result in loan default.

Pros and Cons

Advantages

  • No monthly mortgage payments required
  • Proceeds are generally tax-free
  • You retain home ownership
  • FHA insurance protects against owing more than home value
  • Flexible payment options

Disadvantages

  • Reduces inheritance for heirs
  • High upfront costs and fees
  • Interest compounds over time
  • Must maintain property taxes and insurance
  • Can affect eligibility for certain government programs

Payment Options

Payment Type Description Best For
Lump Sum Receive all proceeds at closing Paying off existing mortgage or large expenses
Monthly Payments Fixed monthly payments for life (tenure) or set period (term) Supplementing monthly retirement income
Line of Credit Access funds as needed, unused portion grows Emergency fund or flexible spending needs
Combination Mix of monthly payments and line of credit Income supplement with emergency access

Who Should Consider a Reverse Mortgage?

Good Candidates

  • Planning to stay in home long-term
  • Need additional retirement income
  • Have significant home equity
  • Can afford ongoing home expenses

Poor Candidates

  • Planning to move within a few years
  • Want to leave home as inheritance
  • Cannot afford property taxes and insurance
  • Have other affordable financing options

Costs and Fees

Upfront Costs Can Be Significant

Alternatives to Consider

Downsizing

Sell your home and move to a smaller, less expensive property to access equity.

HELOC

Home Equity Line of Credit allows you to borrow against your home with monthly payments.

Refinancing

Cash-out refinancing lets you access equity while maintaining traditional mortgage payments.
