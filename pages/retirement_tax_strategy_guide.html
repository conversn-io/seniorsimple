Strategic Withdrawal Sequencing

The order in which you withdraw from different accounts can significantly impact your lifetime tax bill.

The Generally Recommended Withdrawal Order

1

  • Minimizes lifetime tax burden

  • Tax bracket management is crucial
  • Healthcare costs may require flexibility

Tax-Loss Harvesting

Offset investment gains with losses to reduce your current year tax liability while maintaining your investment strategy.

How Tax-Loss Harvesting Works

1

Identify Losses

Review your portfolio for investments that are trading below their cost basis

2

Sell Loss Positions

Sell investments at a loss to realize the tax benefit

3

Offset Gains

Use losses to offset capital gains and up to $3,000 of ordinary income

4

Reinvest Wisely

Reinvest proceeds in similar (but not identical) investments

Tax-Loss Harvesting Benefits

Reduces current year tax liability
Unused losses carry forward indefinitely
Maintains investment exposure
Can be repeated annually

Annual Tax Savings Potential

Offset $10,000 in gains: $1,500-$2,400 saved
Offset $3,000 ordinary income: $360-$1,110 saved

Wash Sale Rule - Critical to Understand

What is the Wash Sale Rule?

The IRS disallows the tax loss if you purchase the same or "substantially identical" security within 30 days before or after the sale.

Example: If you sell Apple stock at a loss on January 15, you cannot buy Apple stock again until February 15 (30 days later).

How to Avoid Wash Sales

  • Wait 31 days before repurchasing
  • Buy a similar but different security
  • Use different asset classes (stocks vs bonds)
  • Consider ETFs vs mutual funds
