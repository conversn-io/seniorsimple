Understanding Estate Taxes

Estate taxes can significantly impact the wealth you pass to your heirs. While most estates won't face federal estate taxes due to high exemption thresholds, understanding the rules and implementing proper planning strategies is crucial for protecting your family's financial future.

Important: Sunset Provision

The current high exemption amounts are scheduled to be cut in half in 2026 (approximately $6.8 million per person) unless Congress acts to extend them. This makes current planning opportunities particularly valuable.

How Federal Estate Tax Works

1

Gross Estate Calculation

All assets owned at death are valued at fair market value

2

Deductions Applied

Debts, expenses, and charitable/marital deductions are subtracted

3

Exemption Applied

The unified credit eliminates tax on amounts up to the exemption

4

Tax Calculated

Any remaining amount is taxed at rates up to 40%

State Estate Tax Landscape

While most states don't impose estate taxes, several states have their own estate tax systems with lower exemption amounts than the federal system.

States with Estate Tax

  • Connecticut: $12.92 million exemption
  • Hawaii: $5.49 million exemption
  • Illinois: $4 million exemption
  • Maine: $6.41 million exemption
  • Massachusetts: $2 million exemption
  • Minnesota: $3 million exemption
  • New York: $6.58 million exemption
  • Oregon: $1 million exemption
  • Rhode Island: $1.733 million exemption
  • Vermont: $5 million exemption
  • Washington: $2.193 million exemption
  • District of Columbia: $4.71 million exemption

Additional Considerations

  • Inheritance Tax States: Iowa, Kentucky, Maryland, Nebraska, New Jersey, and Pennsylvania tax beneficiaries
  • Residency Issues: Your state of residence at death determines which state's estate tax applies
  • Multi-State Property: Real estate in estate tax states may be subject to that state's tax
  • Tax Rates: State estate tax rates typically range from 0.8% to 20%

Planning Tip

Consider changing your state of residence if you live in a high estate tax state. However, ensure you meet all requirements for establishing residency in your new state, as states aggressively audit residency claims.

Valuation and Timing Strategies

Optimal Timing Strategies

  • Market Downturns: Transfer assets when values are depressed
  • Pre-Liquidity Events: Gift business interests before IPO or sale
  • Low Interest Rates: Benefit GRATs and installment sales
  • Health Considerations: Accelerate planning if health declines

Valuation Discounts

  • Minority Interest: 10-30% discount for non-controlling interests
  • Marketability: 15-35% discount for illiquid assets
  • Key Person: Discount for dependence on specific individuals
  • Family Limited Partnerships: Structure to maximize discounts

Business Succession Planning

  • Preferred/Common Recaps: Retain control while gifting growth
  • Employee Stock Ownership Plans: Tax-deferred sale proceeds

Real Estate Strategies

  • Delaware Statutory Trusts: 1031 exchange opportunities

Common Estate Tax Planning Mistakes

Waiting Too Long to Plan

Estate tax planning is most effective when done early. Waiting until illness or advanced age limits available strategies.

Ignoring State Estate Taxes

Many people focus only on federal estate taxes and overlook potentially significant state estate tax obligations.

Inadequate Valuations

Failing to obtain proper appraisals for gifted assets can result in missed opportunities or IRS challenges.

Poor GST Allocation

Misallocating GST exemption can waste this valuable tax benefit and create unexpected tax obligations.

Inadequate Liquidity Planning

Failing to plan for estate tax liquidity can force fire sales of assets or borrowing at unfavorable terms.

Incomplete Documentation

Poor documentation of gifts, valuations, and planning decisions can lead to IRS disputes and family conflicts.

Neglecting Plan Updates

Estate plans must be updated for changes in family circumstances, tax laws, and asset values.

DIY Complex Planning

Attempting complex estate planning without professional guidance often results in costly mistakes.
